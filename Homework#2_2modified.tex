2 Kernels and RKHS\\
\vspace{.4cm}
1 Find the RKHS over $\mathbb{R}^d$ for each of the following kernels.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x,y)=x^{\top}y+c,\hspace{.5cm} x,y\in\mathbb{R}^d, c\in \mathbb{R}^{+}$.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Notice that $K(x_i,x_j)$ can be written as\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x_i,x_j)=\langle\phi(x_i),\phi(x_i)\rangle$ with $\phi(x)=[x^{\top},\sqrt{c}]^{\top}\in\mathbb{R}^{d+1}$.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Then define H as\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$H=\left\{f_v=\sum\limits_i^n \alpha_i \phi(x_i) \Big| \hspace{.5cm} f_v(x)=v^{\top}\phi(x)\right.$\\
\hspace{1cm} with $\langle f_v,f_w\rangle =\langle \sum\limits_i^n \alpha_i\phi(x_i),\sum\limits_j^m \beta_j \phi(x_j)\rangle=\langle v,w \rangle=$\\
\hspace{1cm}$\sum\limits_i^n\sum\limits_j^m \alpha_i \beta_j\langle\phi(x_i),\phi(x_i)\rangle=\sum\limits_i^n\sum\limits_j^m \alpha_i \beta_j K(x_i,x_j)$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
*I realize that n must equal m to make any sense.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is a vector space since\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$f_{v+w}=f_{v}+f_{w}$ since $f_{(v+w)}(x)=(v+w)^{\top}\phi(x)=(v^{\top}+w^{\top})\phi(x)=f_{v}(x)+f_{w}(x)$\\
$f_{\alpha v}=\alpha f_{v}$ since $f_{\alpha v}(x)=\alpha v^{\top}\phi(x)=\alpha f_v(x)$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is complete since it over $\mathbb{R}^{d+1}$ which is of finite dimension.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x_i,\cdot)=\phi(x_i)\in H$ with $\alpha_k=\delta_{ki}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
K has the reproducing property since\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$\langle f_v,K(x,\cdot)\rangle=v^{\top}\phi(x)=f_v(x)$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Thus H is a RKHS.\\
\pagebreak
$K(x_i,x_j)=\left(x_i^{\top}x_j\right)^2$\hspace{1cm}$x_i \in\mathbb{R}^d$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Notice that $K=\left(x_i^{\top}x_j\right)^2=\left(x_i^{\top}x_j\right)\left(x_i^{\top}x_j\right)=x_i^{\top}\left(x_j x_j^{\top}\right)x_i=x_iBx_i$.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Since B has the form\\
\vspace{.4cm}
\hspace{3cm} $B=$
    ${\begin{bmatrix}
    x_j^1 x_j^1 & x_j^1 x_j^2& \cdots &x_j^1 x_j^d\\
    \vdots&           &                &\vdots\\
    x_j^d x_j^1 & x_j^d x_j^2& \cdots &x_j^d x_j^d
    \end{bmatrix}}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
it is symmetric and thus $K(x_i,x_j)=x_i^{\top}S_Bx_i$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Guess that
\vspace{.4cm}
$H=\left\{f_S \Big| f_S(x)=x^{\top}Sx\right.$ where S is a $d \times d$ symetric matrix\\
\hspace{3cm} with $\langle f_S,f_{S_B}\rangle=\sum\limits_{nm}^d [S]_{nm}[S_B]_{nm}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is a vector space since\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$f_{S_A}+f_{S_B}=f_{S_A+S_B}$ since $x^{\top}S_Ax+x^{\top}S_Bx=x^{\top}(S_A+S_B)x$ and\\
$f_{\alpha S}=\alpha f_S$ since $x^{\top}\alpha Sx=\alpha x^{\top} Sx$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is complete since it is over a space of finite dimensions $d\times d$.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x,\cdot) \in H$ since if we take $K(x_j,\cdot)=f_{S_B}$ then $K(x_i,x_j)=x_i S_B x_i$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
K has the reproducing property since each element of B is $x_j^m x_j^n$ we have\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$\langle f_S,K(x_j,\cdot)\rangle=\langle f_S,f_{S_B}\rangle=\sum\limits_{nm}^d [S]_{nm}[S_B]_{nm}=$
$\sum\limits_{nm}^d x_j^n x_j^m [S]_{nm}=x_j^{\top}Sx_j=f_S(x_j)$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Hence H is a RKHS.
