\section*{1.\quad Expansion via Binomial Theorem}

Applying the binomial theorem with $p=3$:
\[
  K(x,y) = (c + x^\top y)^3
          = c^3 + 3c^2(x^\top y) + 3c(x^\top y)^2 + (x^\top y)^3.
\]
Each power of $x^\top y$ lifts to a tensor inner product.  Using the
Frobenius\,/\,tensor inner product
\[
  \langle A,B\rangle_F = \operatorname{tr}(A^\top B),
  \qquad
  (x^\top y)^k = \langle x^{\otimes k},\, y^{\otimes k}\rangle,
\]
where $x^{\otimes k}=x\otimes\cdots\otimes x$ ($k$ times) is a rank-1
symmetric tensor of order $k$.

\section*{2.\quad Feature Map}

The explicit feature map $\phi:\mathbb{R}^d\to\mathcal{H}$ stacks four
tensor components:
\[
  \phi(x)
  =\begin{pmatrix}
      c^{3/2}\\[4pt]
      \sqrt{3c^2}\;x\\[4pt]
      \sqrt{3c}\;x^{\otimes 2}\\[4pt]
      x^{\otimes 3}
    \end{pmatrix},
\]
where $x^{\otimes 2}=xx^\top\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times d}$ and
$x^{\otimes 3}\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times d\times d}$, both flattened.
The inner product recovers the kernel:
\[
  \langle\phi(x),\phi(y)\rangle
  = c^3 + 3c^2(x^\top y) + 3c(x^\top y)^2 + (x^\top y)^3
  = K(x,y).\quad\checkmark
\]

\section*{3.\quad The RKHS as a Tensor Space}

Every $f\in\mathcal{H}_K$ is parametrised by $(a,b,C,\mathcal{T})$ with
\[
  a\in\mathbb{R},\quad
  b\in\mathbb{R}^d,\quad
  C\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times d},\quad
  \mathcal{T}\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times d\times d},
\]
and evaluated as:
\begin{mathbox}
\[
  f(x) = a + b^\top x + \operatorname{tr}(C^\top xx^\top)
        + \langle \mathcal{T},\, x^{\otimes 3}\rangle.
\]
\end{mathbox}

The cubic term contracts $\mathcal{T}$ against $x^{\otimes 3}$.
Using the mode-1 matricisation $\mathcal{T}_{(1)}\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times d^2}$:
\[
  \langle \mathcal{T},\,x^{\otimes 3}\rangle
  = x^\top \mathcal{T}_{(1)}(x\otimes x).
\]
This yields a fully matrix-algebraic evaluation with no explicit index loops.

%--------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{4.\quad RKHS Inner Product}

For $f\leftrightarrow(a,b,C,\mathcal{T})$ and
$g\leftrightarrow(a',b',C',\mathcal{T}')$:
\begin{mathbox}
\[
  \langle f,g\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
  = \frac{aa'}{c^3}
  + \frac{b^\top b'}{3c^2}
  + \frac{\langle C,C'\rangle_F}{3c}
  + \langle\mathcal{T},\mathcal{T}'\rangle_F,
\]
\end{mathbox}
where the tensor Frobenius inner product is
$\langle\mathcal{T},\mathcal{T}'\rangle_F
 =\operatorname{tr}(\mathcal{T}_{(1)}^\top\mathcal{T}'_{(1)})$.
The RKHS norm is therefore:
\[
  \|f\|_{\mathcal{H}}^2
  = \frac{a^2}{c^3}
  + \frac{\|b\|^2}{3c^2}
  + \frac{\|C\|_F^2}{3c}
  + \|\mathcal{T}\|_F^2.
\]

%--------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{5.\quad Representer in Tensor Form}

The kernel section $K(\cdot,x)$---the representer of evaluation at
$x$---corresponds to:
\[
  K(\cdot,x)\;\leftrightarrow\;
  \bigl(c^3,\;\;3c^2 x,\;\;3c\,xx^\top,\;\;x^{\otimes 3}\bigr).
\]
The reproducing property is verified cleanly:
\begin{align*}
  \langle f,K(\cdot,x)\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
  &= \frac{a\cdot c^3}{c^3}
   + \frac{b^\top(3c^2 x)}{3c^2}
   + \frac{\langle C,3c\,xx^\top\rangle_F}{3c}
   + \langle\mathcal{T},x^{\otimes 3}\rangle_F\\[4pt]
  &= a + b^\top x + x^\top Cx + \langle\mathcal{T},x^{\otimes 3}\rangle
   = f(x).\quad\checkmark
\end{align*}

%--------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{6.\quad Kernel Matrix (No Loops)}

On a dataset $X=[x_1|\cdots|x_n]\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times n}$, define the
shifted Gram matrix $\mathbf{G}=c\,\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^\top+X^\top X$.
The full kernel matrix is the Hadamard cube:
\begin{mathbox}
\[
  \mathbf{K} = \mathbf{G}\circ\mathbf{G}\circ\mathbf{G}.
\]
\end{mathbox}
No loops required---assembled from one BLAS matrix multiply and two
elementwise products.

\section*{7.\quad General Degree-$p$ Pattern}

The structure generalises to $K(x,y)=(c+x^\top y)^p$ via the binomial
theorem:
\[
  K(x,y)
  =\sum_{k=0}^{p}\binom{p}{k}c^{p-k}
    \langle x^{\otimes k},y^{\otimes k}\rangle.
\]

\medskip
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{cccc}
\toprule
Degree $k$ & Feature & Weight in $\|f\|_{\mathcal{H}}^2$ & Parameter \\
\midrule
$0$ & $1$ & $c^{-p}$ & $a\in\mathbb{R}$ \\[2pt]
$1$ & $x$ & $\displaystyle\left[\tbinom{p}{1}c^{p-1}\right]^{-1}$ & $b\in\mathbb{R}^d$ \\[8pt]
$2$ & $x^{\otimes 2}$ & $\displaystyle\left[\tbinom{p}{2}c^{p-2}\right]^{-1}$ & $C\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times d}$ \\[8pt]
$\vdots$ & $\vdots$ & $\vdots$ & $\vdots$ \\[2pt]
$k$ & $x^{\otimes k}$ & $\displaystyle\left[\tbinom{p}{k}c^{p-k}\right]^{-1}$ & $\mathcal{T}^{(k)}\in\mathbb{R}^{d^k}$ \\[8pt]
$p$ & $x^{\otimes p}$ & $1$ & $\mathcal{T}^{(p)}\in\mathbb{R}^{d^p}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\medskip
The RKHS is the space of \textbf{polynomials of degree $\leq p$} on
$\mathbb{R}^d$, with geometry determined entirely by symmetric tensor
algebra and Frobenius inner products---no index gymnastics needed.
