2 Kernels and RKHS\\
\vspace{.4cm}
1 Find the RKHS over $\mathbb{R}^d$ for each of the following kernels.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x,y)=x^{\top}y+c,\hspace{.5cm} x,y\in\mathbb{R}^d, c\in \mathbb{R}^{+}$.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Notice that $K(x,y)$ can be written as\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x,y)=(y^{\top},\sqrt{c})(x^{\top},\sqrt{c})^{\top},\hspace{.5cm} x^{\prime},y^{\prime} \in \mathbb{R}^{d+1}, c\in \mathbb{R}^{+}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Guess that\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$H=\left\{f_{v,v_0}; f_{v,v_0}(x)=(v^{\prime})^{\top} x^{\prime}=(v^{\top},\sqrt{c})(x^{\top},\sqrt{c})^{\top}-c+v_0 \hspace{.5cm} v^{\prime},x^{\prime}\in \mathbb{R}^{d+1},v_0 \in \mathbb{R} \right.$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is a vector space. Let all vectors in $\mathcal{X}$ now be augmented $\in \mathbb{R}^{d+1}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$f_{(v,v_0)+(w,w_0)}=f_{(v,v_0)}+f_{(w,w_0)}$ since $f_{(v,v_0)+(w,w_0)}(x)=(v+w)^{\top}x+v_0+w_0=(v^{\top}+w^{\top})x+v_0+w_0=f_{(v,v_0)}(x)+f_{(w,w_0)}(x)$\\
$f_{\alpha(v,v_0)}=\alpha f_{(v,v_0)}$ since $f_{\alpha(v,v_0)}(x)=\alpha v^{\top}+ \alpha v_0=\alpha f_{\alpha(v,v_0)}(x)$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
K(x,y) is in H.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x,y)=x^{\top}y=y^{\top}x=f_{(y,y_0=0)}\hspace{1cm} x,y \in \mathbb{R}^{d+1}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is also an inner product space.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$\langle f_{v,v_0},g_{w,w_0}\rangle=\sum\limits_{ij}\alpha_i\beta_j x_{i}^{\top}y_j=\left(\sum\limits_{i}^{n} \alpha_i x_i\right)^{\top}\left(\sum\limits_{j}^{m} \beta_j y_j\right)$\\
$=v^{\top}w$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Also H has a norm iduced from the inner product\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$\parallel f_{v,v_0} \parallel=v^{\top}v=\parallel v\parallel^2$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Since $x \in \mathbb{R}^d$ for finite d, H is complete. Hence H is a Hilbert space.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
By this construction of H, it has the reproducing property because\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$f_{v,v_0=0}(y)=v^{\top}y \implies f_{v,v_0=0}=v^{\top}$ and\\
$\langle f_{v,v_0},K(y,\cdot)\rangle=\langle f_{v,v_0},f_{(y,y_0=0)}\rangle=v^{\top}y$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Thus H is RKHS.\\

2. $K(x_i,x_j)=(x_i^{\top}x_j)^2$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Notice if we write $x_i^{\top}x_j=(x_i^{\top} I x_j)$ where I is the identity matrix,\\
then this is a bilinear form. Any bilinear form can be expressed\\
as a quadratic form; with $x_i \equiv x$ as $K=x_i^{\top} P^{\top} I P x_i = x P^{\top} P x=x^{\top} A x$,\\
$A \equiv P^{\top}P$. Now substituting this expression into K gives\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x_i,x_j)=(x^{\top}Ax)(x^{\top}Ax)=x^{\top}A (x x^{\top})Ax=x^{\top}A^{\top}BAx$ with $B\equiv x x^{\top}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Now for $x \in\mathbb{R}^d$, guess that H is\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$\left\{f_S: f_S(x)=x^{\top}Sx\right.$ where S is a symmetric $d\times d$ matrix with\\
$\langle f_{S_A},f_{S_B}\rangle \equiv \langle S_A,S_B\rangle=\sum\limits_{ij}^d[S_A]_{ij}[S_B]_{ij}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is a vector space since\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$f_{S_A}+f_{S_B}=f_{S_A+S_B}$ since $x^{\top}S_Ax+x^{\top}S_Bx=x^{\top}(S_A+S_B)x$ and\\
$f_{\alpha S}=\alpha f_S$ since $x^{\top}\alpha Sx=\alpha x^{\top} Sx$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
H is complete since it is over a space of finite dimensions $d\times d$.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$K(x,\cdot) \in H$ since for $S_B \equiv A^{\top}BA$ we have
\vspace{.4cm}
$k(x_i,x_j)=x_i^{\top} S_B x_i \implies$\\
$K(x_i,\cdot)=f_{S_B}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
K also has the reproducing property.\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$\langle f_s ,K(x,\cdot)\rangle=\langle f_S,f_{S_B}\rangle=\sum\limits_{ij}^d [S]_{ij}[S_B]_{ij}=$\\
$\sum\limits_{ij}[S]_{ij}[A^{\top}BA]_{ij}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Now $B=xx^{\top}$ and is a symmetric pos. definite matrix and so $A^{\top}BA=B$,\\
takes the form below. If we allow  postscripts to represent the components of the \\
vector $x$,i.e $x=(x^1,x^2,...,x^d)$, then $A^{\top}BA$ takes the form\\
\vspace{.4cm}
\hspace{2cm} $A^{\top}BA=$
    ${\begin{bmatrix}
    x^1 x^1 & x^1 x^2& \cdots &x^1 x^d\\
    \vdots&          &          &\vdots\\
    x^d x_i^1 & x^d x^2& \cdots &x^d x^d
    \end{bmatrix}}$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
The matrix composed of products of the components of the same vector $x_i$.\\
Then $[B]_{mn}=x^m x^n$ and so\\
\vspace{.4cm}
$\langle f_s ,K(x_i,\cdot)\rangle=\sum\limits_{mn}^d[B]_{mn}[S]_{mn}=\sum\limits_{mn}^d x^m x^n[S]_{mn}\Big|_{x=x_i}=x_i^{\top}Sx_i=f_S(x_i)$\\
\vspace{.4cm}
Thus H is a RKHS with the kernel K.\\
